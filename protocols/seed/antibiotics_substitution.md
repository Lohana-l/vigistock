# Antibiotics: shortage substitution quick reference

Scope: intravenous antibiotics commonly dispensed in hospital settings,
ATC class **J01**.

## Beta-lactams

### Piperacillin-tazobactam (J01CR05) shortage
- Alternatives: cefepime (J01DE01) + metronidazole (J01XD01) for
  polymicrobial coverage; meropenem (J01DH02) when ESBL suspected.
- Posology adjustment: see local antibiogram + renal clearance.

### Ceftriaxone (J01DD04) shortage
- Alternative: cefotaxime (J01DD01), 2 g q 8 h for most adult
  indications.
- Contraindication awareness: hyperbilirubinaemia in neonates differs
  between the two.

## Carbapenems

### Meropenem shortage
- Alternative: imipenem-cilastatin (J01DH51) 500 mg q 6 h. Lower seizure
  threshold in patients with renal impairment or CNS lesions: avoid if
  contraindicated.

## Cold-chain

Most IV antibiotics do NOT require refrigeration before reconstitution.
Cold-chain flags on antibiotic lots typically indicate an
operational error rather than a clinical risk.
