# Insulin: shortage substitution guidance

## 1. Scope

Applies to basal and rapid-acting insulin preparations dispensed from
hospital pharmacy stocks (ATC codes A10AB / A10AC / A10AD / A10AE).

## 2. Acceptable substitutions within class

### 2.1 Basal insulin (A10AE)
- Insulin glargine U100 to Insulin detemir: unit-for-unit conversion
  acceptable for stable patients; endocrinology consult recommended for
  type 1 diabetes.
- Insulin degludec: start with 80 % of total daily dose when switching
  from glargine U100 due to longer half-life.

### 2.2 Rapid-acting (A10AB)
- Insulin lispro, insulin aspart and insulin glulisine: 1:1 unit
  conversion, no titration required.

## 3. Cold-chain handling

Unopened insulin vials/pens must be stored 2-8 °C. Once in use, vials
may be kept at room temperature for up to 28 days (manufacturer-specific;
check product label).

Cold-chain excursion above 8 °C for more than 4 h (or > 15 °C at any
point) requires quarantine and manufacturer consultation before clinical
use.

## 4. Redistribution

Insulin lots are subject to refrigerated shipping only. Redistribution
between hospitals requires:

- Unbroken temperature log.
- Receiving pharmacist sign-off.
- Regulatory documentation (decree 2023-XYZ in France).
